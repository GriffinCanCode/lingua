# Russian Language Curriculum

**Target:** B2+ Proficiency (Upper-Intermediate)  
**Total Units:** 25  
**Estimated Duration:** 12-18 months of regular study

---

## Design Principles

- **Serious learners** heading for real fluency
- **STRESS MARKS on all words** - Russian stress is unpredictable and critical
- **Full Cyrillic fluency** - no transliteration crutches past Unit 2
- **Culture heavily integrated** - topics drive vocabulary selection
- **Hybrid grammar** - explicit rules delivered through patterns and examples
- **Verbs from Unit 1** - conjugation is non-negotiable for real fluency

---

## Proficiency Milestones (CEFR)

| Units | Level | Can Do |
|-------|-------|--------|
| 1-5 | A1 | Survival Russian: greet, ask directions, order food, introduce self |
| 6-12 | A2 | Elementary: describe daily routines, past events, make plans |
| 13-18 | B1 | Intermediate: express opinions, tell stories, handle most travel situations |
| 19-25 | B2 | Upper-Int: read news, discuss abstract topics, follow complex arguments |

## Phase 1: Foundations (Units 1-5) — A1

### Unit 1: First Steps
**Lessons:** 16 (including stress intro and pronunciation)  
**Words:** ~95  
**Theme:** First contact with Russian

#### Grammar Focus
- **STRESS MARKS** - Critical foundation (lesson 0)
- **PRONUNCIATION QUIRKS** - Hard sounds & spelling-pronunciation differences (lesson 4)
- Cyrillic alphabet (complete)
- Gender system (masculine/feminine/neuter)
- Personal pronouns (я, ты, он, она́, оно́, мы, вы, они́)
- Zero copula (Я студе́нт = I am a student)
- **Type 1 verbs:** знать, понима́ть, рабо́тать, чита́ть
- **Type 2 verbs:** говори́ть
- **Irregular verbs:** хоте́ть, жить
- Possession: У меня́ есть
- Question words: где, кто, что, как
- Numbers 1-10
- Possessive pronouns: мой/моя́/моё
- Adjective gender agreement

#### Pronunciation Rules (Lesson 4)
- **Hard consonants:** Ж (zh), Ш (sh), Щ (shch), Х (kh)
- **The Ы sound:** Deep 'i' with tongue pulled back
- **ЧТО → ШТО:** что, чтобы, ничто́ pronounced with Ш
- **-ЕГО/-ОГО → -ЕВО/-ОВО:** его́, сего́дня - genitive Г sounds like В
- **Final Г → К:** друг→друк, снег→снек
- **Final devoicing:** хлеб→хлеп, год→гот, муж→муш

#### Lesson Breakdown
0. **Russian Stress** - Why stress matters, vowel reduction, minimal pairs
1. **Cognates & Cyrillic** - А, К, М, О, Т letters, instant vocabulary
2. **Hello & Goodbye** - Greetings (formal/informal), спаси́бо, пожа́луйста
3. **False Friends** - Р=R, С=S, Н=N, В=V
4. **Hard Sounds & Quirks** - Ж, Ш, Щ, Х, Ы, pronunciation rules (ЧТО→ШТО, ЕГО→ЕВО)
5. **Is this a...?** - да, нет, не, question intonation
6. **He, She, It** - Gender system, pronouns он/она́/оно́
7. **I Know, I Understand** - Type 1 verb conjugation (знать, понима́ть)
8. **I Have...** - У меня́ есть construction
9. **I Speak Russian** - Type 2 verb conjugation (говори́ть)
10. **Where, Who, What** - Question words (где, кто, что - remember: ШТО!)
11. **I Want, I Live, I Work** - Irregular verbs (хоте́ть, жить, рабо́тать)
12. **My, Your, Our** - Possessive pronouns with gender agreement
13. **Numbers 1-10** - Counting, number-noun agreement basics
14. **Adjectives** - Gender agreement (большо́й/больша́я/большо́е)
15. **Review & Checkpoint** - Comprehensive assessment

#### Vocabulary Themes
- Pronouns & demonstratives
- Question words
- Basic particles (да, нет, не, и, или, но)
- Location words (тут, там, здесь, вот)
- Greetings (formal & informal)
- Cognates & international words
- Essential nouns (дом, книга, вода, etc.)
- Family basics (мама, папа, брат, сестра)
- First verbs (7 total with full conjugations)
- Time expressions (сейча́с, сего́дня, за́втра, вчера́)

#### Culture
- Russian names (formal vs. first name usage)
- Formal vs. informal address (ты vs. вы)
- Importance of patronymics
